Composing Digital Media Portfolio

Cartoon and Design


Single Panel Comic

First assignment involving 'cartoonizing' oneself utilizing Adobe Illustrator.

Multi Panel Comic

Second assignment using Illustrator to create a multi-panel cartoon with some sort of narrative included.

Magazine Spread

Third assignment using Adobe InDesign to create an 11x17 magazine spread with design in mind.

Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

Technology played a huge role in the creation of these projects most obviously in the fact that we utilized software to make them. Further than that, however, technology had a unique impact on my experience so far as my laptop bit the dust about a week into the class. From this, I decided to obtain an Ipad to do the rest of my work and to last the rest of my college career. This was super useful as I got the new piece of technology right before the second assignment. As you can tell, my second round of cartooning ended up much better than the first. The technology difference from using a mouse on the first project to a pen on the second produced a huge gap in detail. It just goes to show how important the technology we are using is towards the creation of a project.

2. Embrace experimentation

As for experimentation, my first two projects were lacking in this department. I noticed that some of the other people in the classroom were adding some sort of meaning behind their projects, whether that be political or societal. For the third, I wanted to try and implement a societal message within my work, something I had no experience to prior. I think it went well overall, highlighting a problem many celebrities face of having photographers being overbearing in their day to day lives in search of getting a bad or intrusive picture. Overall, I really like the way my third project ended up. It combined a distinct message while still having design elements in mind such as typography, color balance, and alignment.

3.Engage with others in a workshop environment

I found the workhop environment to be the most fun (and useful) part of the class by far for a couple of reasons. First, it allowed me to see everyone else's work and realize what was actually possible given the prompts. For example, after seeing Dominique's first cartoon I realized I could include much more detail on the second assignment if I just put more effort into it. The other main reason I enjoyed the workshopping process was that I got to ask peers questions about technology I was not sure how to use. When I got the Ipad, I knew John was already working with one which made it easy to ask him any questions I had about using Illustrator on an Ipad. This made the transition from laptop to Ipad much easier and resulted in my second project being of higher quality than it would have been without workshop.

4. Practice revision within and across media forms

Being able to revise one's work is super useful in the development of both technical skills and gaining broader design knowledge. The most useful assignment I revised was the third assignment in which we made a magazine spread. Initially, I had created a completely different spread utilizing geometric shapes everywhere to try and create a trendy design I saw from others in the class. This failed quite miserably and I had to go back and essentially start from scratch. Instead of modeling after a classmate's project whose skills were likely out of my reach, I picked a simpler model I found from an online magazine. This was a much easier design to emulate and resulted in a much better looking final product. The act of revising this project did teach me the importance of being honest with oneself and not pursuing designs that are out of one's artistic or technical capabilities.

5. Learn to present your work

As for presenting, I found that sharing my work with those nearest me in our small groups to be quite productive. Everyone was able to provide helpful feedback or sometimes even a different direction to take. They also were able to point out specific details that I had missed during creation. For example, I originally had a picture in the bottom left of the magazine spread that really did not fit with the color palette. I was so focused on the societal message that I completely overlooked how well each picture matched the overall design. A simple switch towards a darker photo matched the color palette much better and ended up with a better final product. All that would not have been possible had I not presented my work to those around me. In the future, I would like to get feedback from the whole class I have just not been confident enough in the work I created to share with everyone. Hopefully, I will feel more confident on the video projects we do as I do have some prior experience in that realm, in which case I will be able to present those project(s).
